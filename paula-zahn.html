Paula Zahn

June 2024 · 3 minute read

Paula Ann Zahn is a newscaster and journalist who has worked for a number of high-profile news networks, including Paula Zahn News, Fox News, CNN, and CBS News.

She was born on February 24, 1956, in Omaha, Nebraska, USA. She also hosts and produces “On the Case with Paula Zahn,” a true crime documentary series.

The net worth of Paula Zahn is unknown. He has a net worth of more than $12 million, according to sources, owing primarily to a successful career in broadcast journalism. She has managed numerous projects throughout her career, and her fortune is expected to grow as she continues her endeavors.

Where was Paula Zahn born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Paula grew up in Canton, Ohio, with her parents and three siblings; her mother was a school teacher, and her father worked in sales, which required them to move frequently, so they relocated to Naperville, Illinois, where she attended Naperville Central High School and competed in several beauty pageants.

Paula attended Stephens College in Columbia, Missouri, where she received a cello scholarship, and she then interned at WBBM-TV, where she gained news industry experience. She earned a journalism degree in 1978 and spent the next ten years working at various local stations such as WFAA-TV, KPRC-TV, WHDH-TV, and KCBS-TV.

Paula then got a job with Paula Zahn News, where she became the anchor of the weekend health show “The Health Show” in 1987. She was promoted to host of the early morning newscast “World News This Morning” after a few months.

Is Paula Zahn Single? Relationship

Zahn married Richard Cohen, a New York City real estate developer, in 1987, and the couple has three children. She announced their divorce after 20 years of marriage due to Zahn’s alleged affair with Paul Fribourg, which was never confirmed.

She also sued Cohen, alleging that he mismanaged her career earnings over a 20-year period, but the court dismissed the claim, stating that it was a matrimonial rather than a commercial dispute.

How did Paula Zahn start her Professional Career?

Zahn was hired by CBS News in 1990 and became co-anchor of the show “CBS This Morning” after joining the network.She also covered various events for the network before transitioning to “CBS Evening News” and contributing to “48 Hours” and “CBS News Sunday Morning.”

She worked for CBS News for nine years before transitioning to cable news as a member of Fox News Channel. Paula hosted the nightly newscast “Fox Report” at Fox and later launched her own news program, “The Edge with Paula Zahn.” She was fired for alleged breach of contract after two years with Fox.

She did, however, become a co-host of the WNET show “SundayArts” in 2008.Her next project was with Investigation Discovery, where she started a true crime documentary series called “On the Case with Paula Zahn,” which she is still working on as a producer to this day.
