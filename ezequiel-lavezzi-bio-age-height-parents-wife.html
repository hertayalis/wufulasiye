Ezequiel Lavezzi Biography

Born on May 3, 1985, Lavezzi initiated his career at Estudiantes (BA) and later joined Genoa, where he was loaned to San Lorenzo before securing a permanent move. Napoli signed him in 2007, leading to his triumph in the 2012 Coppa Italia. Subsequently, Paris Saint-Germain acquired him in 2012 for €26.5 million, and he played a pivotal role in the team’s success, contributing to three Ligue 1 titles, two Coupes de la Ligue, and one Coupe de France. His career concluded with a stint in China, playing for Hebei China Fortune from 2016 to 2019.

Lavezzi represented the Argentina national team from 2007 to 2016, achieving notable milestones such as winning an Olympic gold medal in 2008 and reaching the finals of the 2014 FIFA World Cup.

His international debut for Argentina was against Chile on April 18, 2007. In the 2008 Olympics, Lavezzi scored two crucial goals and played a role in Argentina’s gold-medal victory against Nigeria.

Despite being excluded from the 2010 FIFA World Cup squad, Lavezzi

continued to contribute to the national team, forming key partnerships, including a memorable one-two play with Lionel Messi in a friendly against Brazil in 2010.

Under the management of Sergio Batista, Lavezzi became a regular member of the senior national team, participating in the 2011 Copa América. His contributions were evident, including scoring the opening goal in a pre-tournament friendly against Albania.

Lavezzi’s journey extended to the 2014 FIFA World Cup, where he played a pivotal role in Argentina’s campaign, setting up crucial goals. He also featured in the 2015 Copa América final, which ended in defeat against Chile.

In the 2016 Copa América Centenario, Lavezzi made significant contributions before suffering an injury in the semi-finals. Despite missing the final, Argentina fell short to Chile in a penalty shootout.

Known for his pace, energy, technical prowess, and versatility, Lavezzi’s playing style was dynamic, allowing him to excel in various attacking positions. However, he faced criticism for occasional on-field temperaments and disputes with officials.
