Dog leaves home, scores a seat at Metallica concert

July 2024 · 3 minute read

Explore More

She’s just mutts about heavy metal.

A California dog went viral after she skedaddled from her owner’s abode over the weekend, and nosed her way into a Metallica concert — nabbing a seat for the record-breaking, sold-out gig.

“You might have heard we had a four-legged fan join us for #M72LA!” the band wrote on social media Thursday, alongside a viral photo of the canine crasher, Storm, perched politely in the audience for the Aug. 25 show at SoFi Stadium.

“After a full night taking in the show with her #MetallicaFamily, Storm was safely reunited with her actual family the next day. She had a great time listening to her favorite songs, including ‘Barx Æterna,’ ‘Master of Puppies,’ and ‘The Mailman That Never Comes.’”

Storm, whose owner lives next door to the Inglewood venue, was initially reported to have been abandoned at the concert by the the Animal Hope & Wellness Foundation, who took her to a nearby shelter after the show.

In reality, Storm left her owners’ home on her own and made her way to the stadium just in time to give the heavy metal band a round of a-paws, the San Francisco Chronicle reported.

“She is apparently a huge Metallica fan who decided to sneak out for the concert!” Storm’s owner, Arizbeth Hurtado, joked on PetHelpful, the outlet said.

“We were laughing so hard when we saw she was SITTING IN AN ACTUAL SEAT AT THE CONCERT.”

On Monday, Hurtado also posted a photo of her and Storm cuddling to reassure the public the four-legged escapee was back at home, safe and sound.

But while’s Storm adventure went well, experts warned others looking to take man’s best friend to concerts might be barking up the wrong tree.

“Regardless of what people think, no dog should be taken to a rock concert. It was extremely loud,” Animal Hope & Wellness wrote online.

“You definitely shouldn’t bring your furry friends to the #M72 World Tour. But this dog sure did have her day…” Metallica added in their own statement.

The band is on a two-year world tour promoting their “72 Seasons” album, the Chronicle noted.

The Los Angeles leg broke the SoFi’s single-show attendance record — drawing 80,000 fans to the venue for the Aug. 25 show alone, the venue confirmed.
